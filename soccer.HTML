How soccer started:

Soccer’s history stretches back over 3,000 years, with early forms of ball games appearing in various civilizations. In Mesoamerica, the Aztecs played Tchatali, a team game using a rubber ball, sometimes with ritual significance, including human sacrifice for the losing team . In ancient China, the game Cuju involved kicking a leather ball into a target and is considered the earliest scientifically documented form of a kicking game . Cuju later influenced Japan’s Kemari, a ceremonial game emphasizing skill and teamwork ansi.org ansi.org . Other early versions included Episkyros in Greece and Harpastum in Rome, which involved handling and kicking a ball . Aboriginal Australians also played Marn Gook, a kicking game using balls made from leaves or roots

How Big Is Soccer?

Soccer is the most famous sport globally, with its strongest popularity in Europe, South America, Africa, Asia, and parts of North America. Soccer, also known as football outside the U.S., has an estimated 3.5 billion fans worldwide, making it the most popular sport on the planet . Its simplicity, requiring only a ball and space to play, contributes to its universal appeal . The sport is deeply embedded in the culture of many countries, where it is more than just a game—it is a central part of national identity and social life.

What are the types of soccer?

Tiki-Taka A possession-based style popularized by Spain and Barcelona, Tiki-Taka emphasizes short, quick passes, constant movement, and tight midfield control. Like Ginga, it values rhythm and creativity, but instead of sidesteps and feints, it uses positional play and link-up passing to break down defenses.

Joga Bonito (Beautiful Game) Brazil’s own philosophy, which Ginga embodies, is about playing with joy, improvisation, and self-expression.
